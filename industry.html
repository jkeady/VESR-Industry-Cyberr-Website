The Gaming Industry

What Is The Gaming Industry?

The gaming industry focuses on developing, publishing, and distributing video games across multiple platforms such as consoles, PCs(Computers), and mobile devices. It involves technology, creativity, and storytelling (if it is a story based game). It focuses on generating sales through in-game purchases and the games themselves.

My Role: Creative Director

What does a creative director do?

A creative director in the gaming industry oversees the overall vision and artistic direction of a game. They guide every aspect of the making of games such as its visual style, tone, story, and gameplay mechanics. They work closely with designers, artists, writers, and developers and make key decisions on player experience.

Possible Ethical Issues

An ethical issue that could arise in creative directing is focusing on forcing micro-transactions onto the player to get them to spend more money withing the game, which is manipulative. Other ethical issues to occur could be with how a creative director treats its staff, like overworking them.

Possible Cybersecurity Issues

Data breaches could be a big issue. Hackers could target gaming companies or platforms to steal players' personal and financial information, including login credentials and payment data. Another issue that could arise is with cheating software. Cybercriminals could use malicious software for cheating in multiplayer games which would be unfair to other players.

Best Cybersecurity Practices

Good cybersecurity practices to keep game companies up and running smoothly could be with using strong authentication such as multi-factor authentication to protect player and employee accounts. Regular updates to games could patch vulnerabilities quickly and keep the games from being corrupted. Another good practice would be to limit employee access only to systems specifically in their respective roles. This reduces the risl of internal breaches or accidental leaks.
